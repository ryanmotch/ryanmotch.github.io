Industrial engineering in videogame

In my spare time, I like to play factory games. The core principal of these games is basicly industrial engineering, the goal is to build efficient and productive systems. They are all about maximizing output of products with the least amount of time, resouces, and energy. Machines refine raw materials and combine them to make products, and logistical planing is needed to get them to and from the machines. It is crutial to have a sufficient flow of items for each level of the supply chain in appropriate ratios. As the games progress and demand for items increases, it is necissary to identify bottlenecks and efficiently scale up production in a modular manner, as well as the logistical system's capacity and efficiency. Having enough power production to power the entire factory is another important factor.

On the left is Factorio. For this factories logisics, I opted for a main bus of conveyor belts down the center so each resource is readily avalible at any time. When a resource is needed, it breaks off from the main bus and directs towards the machine block. After produced, the final products make their way back to the center, creating a new line.

On the right is Satisfactory. For this factories logistics, I went for a "distributed" rail network. Each resource mine and machine block has its own station for loading and unloading. A series of signals are needed to prevent trains from crashing into one another.
