Manufacturing an eletric motor development bench

In order to aid a professor with his reaserch, I designed this motor development bench. This bench is being used to test and configure 3 eletric motors and their motor controller for use on an exoskeleton arm. I opted for extruded aluminum for the frame because it's light, easy to work with and fully adjustable. with just an allen key, every section can be adjusted, and more brackets can be added to support more motors or to mount a dynamometer to measure tourque.

First, I imported as many components I could into Onshape and drew the rest of the components using the data sheets to ensure proper fit. I then ordered the aluminum and found the right screws that fit the motor mounting holes. I then cut the extruded aluminum with a bandsaw. For the motor brackets, I used the 3D file to cut the shape of the motor brackets using a CNC router out of an aluminum sheet, unfortunatly due to machine malfunctions, I had to then drill the holes by hand. All that was left was assembly and the project was complete.
