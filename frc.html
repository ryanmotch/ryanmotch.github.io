Robotic mechanisms and pneumatic system

During high school, I was heavily involved with my schools FRC team: 2638 Rebel Robotics from Great Neck South High. I worked on every years robot, but during 2016 I was especially proud of my work.

I lead the design of the shooter mechanism. This shooter had 2 rubber wheels on CIM motors to intake the foam ball, angled to a shooting position with a pneumatic actuator, then once the wheels accelerated to full speed, another pnumatic actuator would push the ball into the wheels, launching the ball 10 feet into the air into the goal. Before building the final aluminum mechanism, we built a wooden prototype to make sure the design would work properly.

Almost every year, I was the lead pneumatic system engineer. I would fit robots with systems that would allow multiple actuators to fire at different pressures simultaneously. Every system comprised of the same basic components of a compressor, pressure regulators, solenoid valves, compressed air storage, saftey and manual release valves and guages for every pressure.
